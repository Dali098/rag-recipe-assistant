## Simple Pasta with Tomato Sauce

**Ingredients (2 servings):**
- 200 g pasta (spaghetti or penne)
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 small onion
- 1–2 garlic cloves
- 200–250 ml tomato sauce or passata
- Salt, black pepper, a pinch of sugar (to taste)
- Dried oregano or basil (optional)
- Grated cheese (optional)

**Steps:**
1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil and add salt (about 1 tablespoon per 2–3 liters of water).
2. Add the pasta and cook until *al dente* (usually 1–2 minutes less than the time on the package).
3. While the pasta is cooking, heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat.
4. Add finely chopped onion and sauté until soft and lightly golden.
5. Add minced garlic and cook for another 30 seconds until fragrant.
6. Pour in the tomato sauce, add salt, pepper, and a pinch of sugar to balance the acidity.
7. Simmer the sauce on low heat for 5–7 minutes. Add oregano or basil if you like.
8. Drain the pasta, keeping a few tablespoons of the starchy cooking water.
9. Transfer the pasta into the pan with the sauce, add a bit of pasta water, and mix well.
10. Serve with grated cheese on top.

## Baked Chicken with Potatoes

**Ingredients (2–3 servings):**
- 500–600 g potatoes
- 400–500 g chicken breast or boneless thighs
- 2–3 tablespoons vegetable or olive oil
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 0.5 teaspoon black pepper
- 1 teaspoon paprika
- Dried garlic or 2–3 fresh garlic cloves
- Any dried herbs (oregano, thyme) to taste

**Steps:**
1. Preheat the oven to 190–200 °C.
2. Wash, peel, and cut the potatoes into small cubes or wedges.
3. Cut the chicken into medium-sized pieces.
4. In a large bowl, combine potatoes and chicken, add oil, salt, pepper, paprika, garlic, and herbs.
5. Mix well so everything is evenly coated with seasoning.
6. Spread the mixture in a single layer on a baking tray.
7. Bake for 30–40 minutes, until the potatoes are soft and the chicken is fully cooked and lightly browned. Stir once or twice during baking.
8. Check doneness with a knife: the potatoes should be tender, and the chicken should have clear juices.

## Oven-Roasted Vegetables

**Ingredients:**
- 1 bell pepper
- 1 small zucchini or courgette
- 1 carrot
- 1 small red onion
- 2–3 tablespoons olive oil
- Salt, black pepper
- Dried herbs (Italian herbs, oregano, rosemary)

**Steps:**
1. Preheat the oven to 190–200 °C.
2. Wash the vegetables and cut them into medium pieces of similar size.
3. Place the vegetables in a bowl, add oil, salt, pepper, and herbs. Mix well.
4. Transfer to a baking tray in a single layer.
5. Roast for 20–30 minutes, until the vegetables are soft and slightly golden on the edges.
6. Serve as a side dish or a light main course.

## Simple Omelette in a Pan

**Ingredients (1–2 servings):**
- 3 eggs
- 2–3 tablespoons milk (optional)
- A pinch of salt
- A pinch of black pepper
- 1 tablespoon butter or oil

**Steps:**
1. In a bowl, lightly beat the eggs with a fork, add salt, pepper, and milk if using.
2. Heat a pan over medium heat and melt the butter or add oil.
3. Pour the egg mixture into the pan.
4. Cook on medium or low heat. When the edges start to set, gently pull them towards the center with a spatula, letting the liquid egg flow underneath.
5. When the omelette is almost set and just slightly runny on top, fold it in half or carefully flip it.
6. Cook for another 30–60 seconds until fully set, then serve.

## Fresh Cucumber & Tomato Salad

**Ingredients (2 servings):**
- 2 medium tomatoes
- 1 cucumber
- 1–2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 teaspoon lemon juice or vinegar
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Fresh herbs (dill, parsley, or basil) optional

**Steps:**
1. Wash the vegetables and cut tomatoes into wedges, cucumber into thin half-moons.
2. Combine them in a bowl.
3. Add olive oil, lemon juice (or a splash of vinegar), salt, and pepper.
4. Mix gently, taste, and adjust seasoning.
5. Optionally add chopped herbs for freshness.
6. Serve immediately as a side or light meal.

## Basic Steamed Rice

**Ingredients (2 servings):**
- 1 cup white rice
- 2 cups water
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1 teaspoon butter or oil (optional)

**Steps:**
1. Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clearer — this removes excess starch.
2. Add rice, water, and salt to a pot. Add butter/oil if you want.
3. Bring to a boil over medium heat.
4. Once boiling, reduce heat to low, cover with a lid, and cook for 15 minutes without opening the lid.
5. Turn off the heat and let it rest (still covered) for 5–10 minutes — the steam finishes the cooking.
6. Fluff with a fork and serve with vegetables, chicken, or sauce.
